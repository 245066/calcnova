Healthy BMI for Pakistanis — Complete Diet & Fitness Guide 2025

📅 Updated: 2025⏱ 8 min read💪 Health🔍 2,400+ monthly searches
Body Mass Index (BMI) is one of the most used tools to measure healthy weight. But Pakistanis and South Asians have different risk thresholds than Western standards. This guide explains healthy BMI ranges for Pakistanis, health risks, and practical diet and exercise advice you can follow in Pakistan.

🔢 What is BMI and How is it Calculated?

BMI = Weight (kg) divided by Height squared (m²). Example: 70kg at 1.75m = BMI of 22.9 which is healthy.

📐 BMI Formula

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m)  —  Use our free BMI calculator for instant results.

📊 BMI Ranges — Standard vs Pakistani / South Asian

WHO publishes separate BMI guidelines for South Asians. We develop diabetes and heart disease at lower BMI values than Western populations.

⚠️ Important: A BMI of 25 is "overweight" by South Asian standards, not "normal". Pakistanis should aim for BMI under 23 for optimal health.

😷 Why Are Pakistanis More at Risk?

South Asians store more fat around the abdomen (visceral fat) which is more dangerous. Pakistan has one of the highest Type 2 Diabetes rates in the world, directly linked to high BMI and diet habits.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is healthy BMI for Pakistani women?+
For Pakistani women, BMI 18.5–22.9 is healthy. Above 23 is overweight by South Asian standards. Women naturally have more body fat, so waist circumference is also a useful measurement.
Is BMI 25 normal for a Pakistani?+
By South Asian guidelines, BMI 25 is actually overweight for Pakistanis. The healthy range is 18.5–22.9. BMI 25 increases diabetes and heart disease risk significantly for South Asians.
How to lose weight in Pakistan without a gym?+
Walk 30 min daily, reduce portion sizes (especially rice and naan), cut sweet drinks, eat more vegetables and daal, and sleep 7–8 hours. Simple lifestyle changes produce real results.
Is Pakistani food bad for BMI?+
Not at all! Traditional daal, sabzi, and roti is healthy. The problem is too much oil, fried snacks, and sugary drinks. Home-cooked simple Pakistani food with controlled portions is excellent for health.
