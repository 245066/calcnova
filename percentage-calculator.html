📐 Percentage Calculator Formula

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
Three variants: find %, find the part, or find percentage change

The percentage formula has three common variations depending on what you want to find. All three are based on the same fundamental relationship between the part, whole, and percentage rate.

Variant 1 — Find the part: Part = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Whole
Variant 2 — Find percentage change: Change% = ((New − Old) ÷ |Old|) × 100
Variant 3 — Find what percent: Percent = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

📋 How to Calculate Percentage Calculator Manually

  • 1
    Identify what type of percentage calculation you need: finding a part, finding % change, or finding what percent something is.
  • 2
    For "X% of Y": Divide X by 100 then multiply by Y. Example: 25% of 200 = 0.25 × 200 = 50.
  • 3
    For "% change": Subtract old from new, divide by old value, multiply by 100. A positive result is increase, negative is decrease.
  • 4
    For "X is what % of Y": Divide X by Y then multiply by 100. Example: 50 is what % of 200 = (50÷200)×100 = 25%.

📊 Calculation Example

📋 Example 1 — Finding a Part

Question: What is 15% of PKR 8,500?

Formula: (15 ÷ 100) × 8,500 = 0.15 × 8,500

Result: PKR 1,275

📋 Example 2 — Percentage Increase

Question: Salary increased from PKR 80,000 to PKR 95,000. What is the % increase?

Formula: ((95,000 − 80,000) ÷ 80,000) × 100 = (15,000 ÷ 80,000) × 100

Result: 18.75% increase

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage on a calculator?+
To find X% of Y: multiply X by Y then divide by 100. Most calculators have a % button. On our online calculator, just enter X and Y in the first mode and click Calculate.
How to calculate percentage increase?+
Percentage increase formula: ((New Value - Old Value) ÷ Old Value) × 100. If the result is positive it is an increase, if negative it is a decrease. Use our Percentage Change mode above.
What is 20% of 1000?+
20% of 1000 = (20 ÷ 100) × 1000 = 0.20 × 1000 = 200. Use our X% of Y calculator above for any percentage calculation instantly.
How to calculate discount percentage?+
Discount % = ((Original Price - Sale Price) ÷ Original Price) × 100. For example, if original price is Rs. 2,000 and sale price is Rs. 1,500, discount = ((2000-1500)÷2000)×100 = 25% discount.
How to calculate percentage marks?+
To calculate your percentage marks: (Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100. For example, 850 out of 1100 = (850÷1100)×100 = 77.27%. Use our CGPA calculator for university grades.
